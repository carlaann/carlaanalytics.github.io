About Me

I am medical Technologist (clinical laboratory scientist) pursuing a master’s in data science. Working in healthcare for many years, I always imagined that I would retire in my current field until I saw a billboard ad for data scientist. It made me very curious and I researched the information. In the beginning, I thought it was like being a scientist. After researching, I said this career can help me with most of the things I love to do.
As a kid, I always had a big imagination and wanted to make a difference in the world. For the most part, I pursuedmost of those imaginations and was successful at quite a few. After graduating with a Bachelor of Science in Medical Technology which is Clinical Laboratory Scientist, I was always experimenting on ideas and created a logo for National Laboratory Week in New Orleans for national laboratory week. Over 400 t shirts were sold on the first attempt. I thought it was funand continue to pursue the fashion industry after a friend inspired her to investigate designing t-shirts with the use of rhinestones. This eventually led to a meeting with a sales representative from Swarovski. After receiving an account with Swarovski along with my first order, I devised a more effective and efficient process for producing stunning t-shirts encrusted with rhinestone which led to a corporate account of designing t-shirts for Elizabeth Arden which led me to creating promotional t-shirts for Britney Spears, Mariah Carey and Elizbeth Arden’s fragrance lines produced by Elizabeth Arden.
Business was going so well, and she decided to open a storefront. The renovations to the store were completed. The inauguration for the store had been scheduled four days after Hurricane Katrina and never got the chance to open. Along with the destruction of the store she was forced to relocate to Dallas, Texas, and work under her website www.therhinestoneartist.com.
After making Dallas my place of residence after Katrina, I was driving to work and saw a kid walking to school while snowing with no coat. This kid made me call Sir Earl Toon who is my husband to go to the school to give the kid a coat. Unfortunately, we were unable to find the kid. So, we hosted a coat drive for the whole school and later the dance fitness community within the Dallas Fort Worth are became a part of a traditional event to raise coats for kid. Today, we are called Give A Kid A Coat and provided over 5000 coats to kids in the Dallas Fort Worth area.
